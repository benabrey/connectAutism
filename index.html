Behaviour Consultation, Behaviour Intervention, & Training

What We Do

Behaviour analysis is a research-based, scientific approach to understanding and supporting human behaviour. It's founded on the principle that behaviour is learned over time and shaped by the environment around us. As Behaviour Consultants and Interventionists, we don't judge children's behaviour — we seek to understand the factors influencing it, identify its function, and develop strategies that support positive, meaningful change.

Our goal is to reduce stress and anxiety within family environments while fostering positive relationships among children, parents, siblings, caregivers, teachers, educational assistants, and others who interact with your child. We look at the whole picture — not just the behaviour itself, but the context surrounding it — and build practical strategies that respect your child and your family.

Working together as a team, we identify behavioural goals, monitor and evaluate progress, and adjust intervention strategies as needed. We're committed to supporting families in achieving the goals we establish together, every step of the way.

Registry of Autism Service Providers (RASP) – Government of British Columbia

The Registry of Autism Service Providers (RASP) in British Columbia helps families find qualified professionals such as behaviour consultants, speech-language pathologists, occupational therapists, and physical therapists who can provide autism-related services funded through BC's Autism Funding program.

Learn More
Toilet Training for Everyone – ACT Autism Community Training

This video series by Dr. Pat Mirenda provides practical strategies and behavioural approaches for toilet training children, including children with an ASD diagnosis. The resource covers myths about toilet training, rapid toilet training methods, less intensive approaches, and solutions to common toileting challenges.

Learn More
Justification for Equipment and Supplies Form – Government of British Columbia

A Justification for Equipment (JFE) is a BC Autism Funding form used to request approval for equipment and supplies related to a child's autism intervention program. The form requires information about the recommended items, their costs, developmental goals they support, and professional justification explaining how the items will benefit the child's therapy and development.

Meet the Team

Bonnie McGill, M.Ed. PhD. Candidate

Behaviour Consultant

Bonnie holds a Bachelor of Arts in Psychology from the University of British Columbia, a Master of Education in Curriculum and Instruction with a specialization in Applied Behaviour Analysis (ABA) from Arizona State University, and is currently pursuing a PhD in Organizational Leadership at Adler University.

Bonnie's education and professional practice are grounded in the principles of Applied Behaviour Analysis (ABA), with a focus on Natural Environment Teaching (NET). She integrates evidence-based strategies and positive reinforcement into children's everyday play, routines, and natural environments to build meaningful communication, social, and daily living skills. Bonnie is passionate about educating, training, and mentoring parents, empowering them to foster healthy, meaningful relationships with their children diagnosed with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) and other neurodivergent conditions.

Outside of work, Bonnie enjoys road cycling, open-water swimming, and spending time with her Labradoodle, Poppy.
