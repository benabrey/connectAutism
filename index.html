Behaviour Consultation, Behaviour Intervention, & Training

Purpose of Consultation

Behaviour analysis is a research-based, scientific approach to understanding and supporting human behaviour. It is founded on the principle that behaviour is learned over time and is influenced by direct and indirect consequences within an individual's environment.

As Behaviour Consultants and Interventionists, we do not judge children's behaviour. Instead, we seek to understand the environmental factors that may be influencing the behaviour, identify its function, and develop strategies to shape and support positive behavioural change.

Our goal is to reduce stress and anxiety within family environments while fostering positive relationships among children, parents, siblings, caregivers, teachers, educational assistants, and others who interact with the child.

We are committed to supporting families in achieving the goals we establish together. However, we cannot guarantee specific outcomes or predict future results, as many variables influence a child's development and progress.

Working collaboratively as a team, we will identify behavioural goals, monitor and evaluate progress, and adjust intervention strategies as needed.

Registry of Autism Service Providers (RASP) – Government of British Columbia

The Registry of Autism Service Providers (RASP) in British Columbia helps families find qualified professionals such as behaviour consultants, speech-language pathologists, occupational therapists, and physical therapists who can provide autism-related services funded through BC's Autism Funding program.

Learn More

Toilet Training for Everyone – ACT Autism Community Training

This video series by Dr. Pat Mirenda provides practical strategies and behavioural approaches for toilet training children, including children with an ASD diagnosis. The resource covers myths about toilet training, rapid toilet training methods, less intensive approaches, and solutions to common toileting challenges.

Learn More

Justification for Equipment and Supplies Form – Government of British Columbia

A Justification for Equipment (JFE) is a BC Autism Funding form used to request approval for equipment and supplies related to a child's autism intervention program. The form requires information about the recommended items, their costs, developmental goals they support, and professional justification explaining how the items will benefit the child's therapy and development.

Meet the Team

Bonnie McGill, M.Ed. PhD. Candidate

Behaviour Consultant

Bonnie hold a Bachelor of Arts degree in Psychology from the University of British Columbia, a Master of Education in Curriculum and Instruction in Applied Behaviour Analysis (ABA) from Arizona State University, and she is currently working on her PhD in Organizational Leadership from Adler University. Her education and professional practice are grounded in the principles of Applied Behaviour Analysis (ABA), and applying them in a Natural Environment Teaching (NET) by using positive reinforcement into a child's everyday play, routines, and natural surroundings to build functional social, communication, and daily living skills. Bonnie's work focuses on educating, training, and mentoring parents to empower them in developing healthy, meaningful relationships with their children who have been diagnosed with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) and/or other neurodivergent conditions. When she is not in the practice, she enjoys road cycling, ope water swimming, and being with her Labradoodle, Poppy.
